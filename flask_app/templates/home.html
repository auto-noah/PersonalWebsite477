About Me

My name is Noah Wolff and I have been fascinated by computers and engineering for as long as I can remember. From breaking down and trying to fix my older sister's broken calculators to finally dipping my toes into coding during my final years of high school, I have always wondered how things work and how I could make them work.

Now almost 5 years after my first ever "Hello World" program, I am getting close to wrapping up my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science Engineering at Michigan State University and am looking forward to transitioning my classroom knowledge into real-world experience through an internship opportunity.

I have done all of my software design and algorithm courses (not to mention physics and math) and am now focusing on my final courses at university. I am fluent with several coding languages (ARM Assembly, C, C++, Python, SQL, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Java), have used a few libraries (Pandas, Numpy, Matplotlib, wxWidgets), as well as my fair share of developer tools (Docker, GCloud, GitHub, VSCode, Virtual Studio, IntelliJ, CLion, Spyder, CMake, Visual Paradigm).

I am more than ready and eager to be a part of somebody's team, and contribute in every way I can, while actively building a strong foundation for future technical experience.
